<h2><a href="https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/problems/get-minimum-squares0538/1">Get Minimum Squares</a></h2><h3>Difficulty Level : Difficulty: Medium</h3><hr><div class="problems_problem_content__Xm_eO"><p style="text-align: left; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Given a positive integer <strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla699, sans-serif;">n</strong>, find the<strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla699, sans-serif;"> minimum number</strong> of perfect squares (square of an integer) that sum up to n.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Note:&nbsp;</strong></span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Every positive integer can be expressed as a sum of square numbers since </span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">1 is a perfect square, and any number can be represented as</span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;"> 1*1 + 1*1 + 1*1 + ....</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Examples:</strong></span></p>
<pre style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Input:</strong> n = 100
<strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Output: </strong>1
<strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Explanation: </strong>100 can be written as [10<sup style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">2</sup>] or [5<sup style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">2</sup> + 5<sup style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">2</sup> + 5<sup style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">2</sup> + 5<sup style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">2</sup>] and the minimum number of perfect squares needed for 100 is 1.</span>
</pre>
<pre style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Input: </strong>n = 6
<strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Output: </strong>3
<strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Explanation: </strong></span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">Only possible way to make sum equals to 6 is [1<sup style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">2</sup> + 1<sup style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">2</sup> + 2<sup style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla699, sans-serif;">2</sup>], so minimum square numbers needed is 3.</span></pre>
