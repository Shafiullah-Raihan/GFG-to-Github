<h2><a href="https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/problems/subset-xor--175953/1">Subset XOR</a></h2><h3>Difficulty Level : Difficulty: Medium</h3><hr><div class="problems_problem_content__Xm_eO"><p dir="ltr" style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px 0px 10px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; font-size: 18px; vertical-align: baseline; color: rgb(39, 50, 57); font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif; letter-spacing: 0.162px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;">Given an positive integer&nbsp;</span><strong style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><strong style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">n</strong></strong><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;">, find a subset of numbers from 1 to n (inclusive), where each number can be used at most once, such that:</span></p>
<ul style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;">
<li dir="ltr" style="box-sizing: border-box; border: 0px; font-size: 18px; vertical-align: baseline; color: rgb(39, 50, 57); font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif; letter-spacing: 0.162px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;">The XOR of all elements in the subset is exactly n.</span></li>
<li dir="ltr" style="box-sizing: border-box; border: 0px; font-size: 18px; vertical-align: baseline; color: rgb(39, 50, 57); font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif; letter-spacing: 0.162px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;">The size of the subset is as large as possible.</span></li>
<li dir="ltr" style="box-sizing: border-box; border: 0px; font-size: 18px; vertical-align: baseline; color: rgb(39, 50, 57); font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif; letter-spacing: 0.162px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;">If multiple such subsets exist, choose the <strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">lexicographically smallest</strong> one.</span></li>
</ul>
<p style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><strong data-start="65" data-end="91" style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">Lexicographical Order : </strong>A subset <strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">A[]</strong> is lexicographically smaller than subset&nbsp;<strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">B[]</strong> if at the first index where they differ, <strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><code data-start="195" data-end="208" style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;">A[i] &lt; B[i]</code></strong> (based on character ASCII/Unicode values).<br data-start="251" data-end="254" style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;">If all elements match but one subset ends earlier, the shorter subset&nbsp;is considered smaller.</span></p>
<p style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">Examples:</strong></span></p>
<pre style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">Input: </strong>n = 4<br style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">Output: </strong>[1, 2, 3, 4]</span><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><br style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">Explanation:</strong> We choose all the elements from 1 to 4. Its xor value is equal to n. This is the maximum possible size of the subset.</span></pre>
<pre style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 18px; font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">Input: </strong>n = 3<br style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">Output:</strong> [1, 2]<br style="font-family: Nunito, Bangla396, sans-serif;"><strong style="font-family: &quot;Source Sans 3&quot;, Bangla396, sans-serif;">Explanation:</strong> 1 ^ 2 = 3, This is the smallest lexicographical answer possible with maximum size of subset i.e 2.</span></pre>
